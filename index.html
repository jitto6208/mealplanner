About Kitchen Hub

Kitchen Hub is a living recipe collection and pantry tracker built for Asian home cooks. It was designed for a couple in Singapore — one who loves Japanese cuisine, one who loves Chinese, Thai and Taiwanese — and grew into a shareable tool for anyone who cooks this way.

How to use it

  • Recipes tab — browse, filter by cuisine or cook time, add your own dishes, and get the full recipe from Claude AI.
  • Pantry tab — track your staple ingredients. Tap a status badge to mark items as Low or Out. Hit "Top-up list" to see your shopping list at a glance.
  • Add anything — the collection is open. Add your family's recipes and they'll appear for everyone.

The cuisines

The current collection spans Japanese (donburi, ramen, gyudon), Cantonese Chinese (steamed fish, clay pot rice), Thai (green curry, pad kra pao), Taiwanese (lu rou fan, beef noodles) and Vietnamese (lemongrass prawn vermicelli).

Tips for Singapore cooks

  • Wet market (Compass One or Sengkang) for fresh proteins and veg — Saturday mornings are best.
  • FairPrice / Cold Storage for pantry replenishment and Japanese/Thai specialty items.
  • Redmart for heavy dry goods on a recurring schedule (rice, soy sauce, coconut milk).
  • A 90-minute Sunday batch prep session — pre-cooked rice, marinated eggs, julienned ginger — makes weeknight cooking feel instant.
